Riesling

A wine that is packed full of flavor. It tends to have a taste profile of fruit and is usually picked when grapes are younger. It's higher acidity compliments complex meals that most consumers buy wine for. A good wine to try for occasions but if you do not want white wine, pinot noir is also a light red wine that can compliment meals.

The taste profile changes drastically depending where the wine gape is grown. This is known as "terrior", a french term that describes how the environment changes the taste profile of cultivated foods. This impacts what the grapes create in taste profile year to year. Even a type of grape will change drastically given the environment conditions.

Great Red Wines

If you are unsure where to start, these are great choices.

Pinot Noir

A light red wine that has a taste profile of berries and low tannins. When choosing a complex meal, a simple easy going wine can be the best choice for your food. Many regions grow and cultivate their grapes in certain ways that change the taste of wine. Overall, pinot noir remains a great starter wine for many.

Box Wine

There are many benefits to using and storing wine these days in a box format. Typically all wines suffer from oxidation and suffer from storage issues. A box wine provides you with the ability to minimize air in the wine after closing it. Besides it being more compact, it is a great way to provide wine for larger groups. It should be considered as a great option despite it not being in a bottle.

Susumaniello

This wine has a recently shift in both production and cultivation the last few years. Similarily to California Zinfandel, primitivo wine is being being cultivated and harvested in smaller bushels. This practice is leading to a better tasting profile. Primitivo wine is seen as a lesser wine, but given the change in how smaller the yieds have become, the quality is astounding. This is similar in how high priced wines are grown.
